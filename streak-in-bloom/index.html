A streak, in bloom

Most apps mark your streak on a calendar of little flames. One grows it as a flower.

scroll ↓

One petal is one day

A whole month, clockwise from noon. Hover a petal to read its day.

Grow a month

Drag a day to set its practice. Tap a short one to spend a freeze. Hover any petal.

A calendar streak knows only two things: you showed up, or you didn't. So people game it — one sleepy review at 11:55pm to keep the chain.

The flower won't let you. Height is the time you put in; colour is what you actually learned — so a real day burns tall and deep, and a lazy one stays short and pale. “An app that doesn't punish free learning,” someone called it.

(That “1.1m” in the middle? Months, not millions — about 33 days.)

What people made of it

I showed it to a few thousand learners. They split — and both sides were right.

“That's beautiful, I love it.”
— a learner, on first sight
“Prettier — but more confusing than the calendar.”
— the honest other half
“It reminded me of what remains after I sharpen a pencil.”
— and once you see it, you can't unsee it

That's the point: it trades the calendar's instant clarity for something you'd actually want to look at.

A streak worth growing — not a chain you're scared to break.

The flower lives in Severo, a language app I'm building. If you'd like a rose of your own, it's there.
